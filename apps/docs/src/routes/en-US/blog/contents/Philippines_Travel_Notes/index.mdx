# In conclusion

It felt more like a trip to the Philippines than a wedding trip. Since it was my first time attending a wedding and traveling abroad, I was very worried, but in the end, I had a great time. The toilet issue I was initially concerned about was just me worrying too much.

By knowing the outside world, I was able to re-recognize the obvious fact that I can evaluate my own world. Just as knowing other companies allows you to evaluate your own company, knowing foreign countries allows you to evaluate your own country.

Now, I'm going to share a very personal opinion. I think Japanese people strictly adhere to time, work seriously, and have a culture of living in harmony. Also, in Japan, I think it's good that general education like elementary and middle school is equally provided. Everyone has an equal chance to become anything.

What surprised me in the Philippines was that many people could speak not only their native language (Tagalog) but also English. I heard that it's normal to be able to speak multiple languages. I also heard that many people in Hong Kong can handle multiple languages. In Japan, we also learn a foreign language (English) in school, but the acquired ability seems to be low.

In the Philippines, where the gap between the rich and the poor is extreme, even the poorest people always had a smartphone. I found it very strange. I think they are probably using old smartphones, but I was puzzled about how they handle electricity and WiFi.

There were many discoveries on my trip to the Philippines, and it made me want to travel abroad again. However, I feel a little anxious if I don't go with someone familiar with the local area, like a tour. Therefore, if I travel abroad next time, I think I will choose from a tour. It was a very enjoyable trip!
